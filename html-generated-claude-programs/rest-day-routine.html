SEC 01 Psoas Foam Roller Release Start Here
Foam Roller Psoas Release Deep Tissue
1 Place foam roller horizontally on the floor. Lie face down, positioning the roller just below your hip bone (ASIS) — slightly inward toward your navel.
2 Support yourself on your forearms in a plank position. Let bodyweight slowly sink into the roller — don't aggressively roll. Find a tender spot and hold.
3 Hold for 30–60 seconds, breathing deeply. On each exhale, let more weight drop into the roller. Tiny side-to-side shifts to explore different spots.
4 Optional active component: Slowly extend the leg on that side straight behind you while compressed — this adds a gentle stretch under the release.
5 Switch sides. Repeat.
⚠ If you feel nerve pain shooting down your leg — you've gone too far inward toward the femoral nerve. Shift the roller slightly outward toward the hip bone. A deep, dull, spreading pressure is normal. Sharp or radiating pain is not.
Time per side 60–90s · EACH SIDE
SEC 02 Hip Capsule & Flexion Supine
01

Supine Knee-to-Chest Hold

Lie on back, pull one knee toward chest with both hands. Keep other leg flat. No contract-relax — purely passive. Breathe into the stretch, let gravity do the work.

60s per leg
02

Figure-4 Piriformis Stretch

Lying on back, cross ankle over opposite knee (figure-4 shape). Pull both thighs toward chest. Flex the top foot to protect the knee. Deep sensation in outer glute.

75s per side
SEC 03 Hip Flexor & Anterior Chain Kneeling
03

Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

Kneel on one knee, front foot flat. Torso upright — don't lean. Tuck your pelvis (tailbone under), then drive hips gently forward. Feel the stretch at the front of the back hip. Keep the tuck throughout.

90s per side
04

Low Lunge / Couch Stretch

Low lunge with back knee on floor, or elevate back foot on a couch/wall for deeper version. Arms relaxed, breathe into the front of the hip. No bouncing.

90s per side
SEC 04 90/90 Passive Hold Seated
05

90/90 Passive Hold

Sit in 90/90 position. Stay on one side — don't switch actively like in your program sessions. Sit tall, lean gently forward over front shin with a flat back. Just breathe and melt. This is the passive version of your program drill.

2 min per side
SEC 05 Adductor & Inner Hip Floor
06

Butterfly Stretch

Seated, soles of feet together, knees fall out. Lean forward with a flat back — don't round. Elbows can gently press knees down. Focus on the inner thigh and groin opening.

2 min total
07

Wide-Legged Forward Fold

Seated straddle, legs wide. Walk hands forward keeping back as flat as possible. Let gravity pull you deeper over 60–90s. Don't pull yourself down — just breathe and release.

90s total
SEC 06 Finish
08

Passive Pike Hold

Seated legs straight, fold forward as deep as comfortable. No effort — just gravity. Hold for 90s breathing deeply. This consolidates everything you've just opened.

90s total

Breathe Into It

Exhale deeply in every hold. Your nervous system releases protective tension on the exhale — earning you extra range for free.

🚫

No Loading

Zero contract-relax, zero PAILs/RAILs. Those create micro-stress that needs recovery. Today is tissue maintenance only.

🔥

If Sore, Skip It

If an area is fatigued from a program session, skip that stretch entirely. A short walk or gentle hip circles is enough.
